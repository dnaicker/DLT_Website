Decentralized Voting App

  • Remote e-voting; where people need not to be present at the polling station instead can cast their vote from any remote location using computers, mobile phones.
  • Conventional systems:
  • • Prone to hacking
  • • Inefficient auditing
  • • Misinterpretation of voter intent
  • • Political biasness
  • • Tempering of software programs
  • • Inefficiency in securing the casted votes
    • Blockchain proved to be a substitute for the conventional approach by making the system unalterable and transparent.

Blockchain

  • • It is practically immutable, as altering information would require copious amounts of computing power
  • • It is transparent, as anyone can view its data.
  • • It is transparent, as anyone can view its data.
  • • It is decentralized, as there is no central authority behind it;
  • • Able to view the history of votes

General Use Cases

  • Allow persons with disabilities to vote without assistance.
  • May be used by people in hospital, long term care facilities or similar institutions.
  • Can be used by people who live abroad and remote areas.
  • Lowers voters travelling time as people can vote from anywhere with an Internet connection.
  • There is a lower risk that votes arrive late at the place of counting.
  • Results can be counted automatically and reduce the incidence of counting errors.

Decentralized Voting dApp

Elections need to be secure and unimpeachable irrespective of the organization. People’s privacy and voting protection must be secured and it should not take too long for votes to be counted, as it upraises concerns.
